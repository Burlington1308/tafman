What distinguishes a suspended ceiling from a traditional ceiling?

A suspended ceiling is an additional ceiling that hangs below the main structural ceiling. Contrast to traditional ceilings, suspended ceilings feature a grid that holds lightweight tiles or panels, offering both aesthetic enhancements and practical advantages, such as better acoustics energy efficiency, and the ability to hide ductwork and wiring.

What advantages do suspended ceilings, drylining, and partitioning provide?

Suspended ceilings come with various benefits, such as improved sound insulation, energy efficiency by minimisng the space that needs heating or cooling, and convenient access to concealed electrical and plumbing systems. They allow for quick aesthetic updates, enhance fire protection, and can greatly enhance room lighting aswell as utilising space.
